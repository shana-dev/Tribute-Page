Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela

1918 - 2013

Paving the path for a new South Africa

Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Mveso, Transkei
  • 1930 - Father dies. Entrusted to Thembu Regent Jongintaba Dalindyebo at the age of 12
  • 1934 - Undergoes initiation and attends Clarkebury Boarding Institute in Engcobo
  • 1939 - Enrolls at the University College of Fort Hare, in Alice - the only black university in South Africa. Meets fellow student, Oliver Tambo and becomes friends with his nephew Kaiser Matanzima
  • 1940 - Expelled from the University of Fort Hare for leading a group of students on a political strike
  • 1942 - Completes his BA at the University of South Africa (UNISA)
  • 1943 - Enrolls for a LLB at the University of Witwatersrand
  • 1944 - Joins the African National Congress and forms the ANC Youth League. Mandela marries his first wife, Evelyn Mase
  • 1948 - Elected National Secretary of the ANC Youth League
  • 1951 - Elected President of the ANC Youth League
  • 1952 - Arrested and charged for violating the Suppression of Communism Act. Opens South Africa's first black law firm with Oliver Tambo
  • 1953 - Devises the M-Plan for the ANC’s future underground operations
  • 1955 - Watches as the Congress of the People at Kliptown adopts the Freedom Charter
  • 1956 - Arrested and later joins 155 others on trial for teason. All are acquitted by 29 March 1961
  • 1958 - Divorces Evelyn Mase and marries Nomzamo Winnie Madikizela
  • 1960 - The ANC is banned, the country’s first State of Emergency is imposed and Mandela is one of thousands of people detained. He burns his pass book
  • 1961 - Goes underground to attend the All-in Conference in Pietermartizburg. The ANC forms its armed wing Umkhonto weSizwe with Mandela as Commander-in-Chief. He sets up High and Regional Commands. Writes a letter from the underground proclaiming “The Struggle is my Life”
  • 1962 - Leaves the country for military training and to garner support for the ANC. Sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1963 - Sent to Robben Island. Mandela returns to Pretoria Local Prison. He appears in court for the first time in what becomes known as the Rivonia Trial.
  • 1964 - Mandela is found guilty of sabotage and sentenced to life imprisonment. Gets the prison number 466/64
  • 1968 - Mother dies after a heart attack, just weeks after she visits him in prison. He is denied permission to attend her funeral
  • 1982 - Moved from an isolated prison in Robben Island to Pollsmoor Prison in the suburbs of Cape Town
  • 1985 - Mandela rejects, through his daughter, Zindzi, South African President PW Botha's offer to release him if he renounces violence. He says that the government must first put an end to apartheid
  • 1988 - He is admitted to Tygerberg Hospital where he is diagnosed with tuberculosis. Mandela is moved to Victor Verster Prison in Paarl where he is held for 14 months in a cottage
  • 1990 - ANC is unbanned. Released after spending 27 years in prison. Mandela is elected ANC Deputy President
  • 1992 - Mandela and Winnie announce their separation
  • 1993 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize with President FW de Klerk
  • 1994 - Mandela votes for the first time in his life. Elected by Parliament as first president of a democratic South Africa. Inaugurated as President of the Republic of South Africa. Launches his autobiography Long Walk to Freedom. Establishes the Nelson Mandela Children's Fund
  • 1998 - Marries Graça Machel on his 80th birthday
  • 1999 - Steps down after one term as President, establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2003 - Establishes the Mandela Rhodes Foundation
  • 2004 - Announces that he will be reducing his public appearances to spend more time with his family and friends
  • 2009 - The United Nations General Assembly declared, 18 July ‘Nelson Mandela International Day’. Attends the inauguration of President Jacob Zuma on 9 May.
  • 2010 - Makes a surprise appearance at the final of the Fifa World Cup in Soweto. His second book "Conversations with Myself" is published
  • 2011 - His book "Nelson Mandela By Himself: The Authorised Book of Quotations" is launched
  • 2012 - Admitted to and discharged from hospital on an ongoing basis. Spends his 95th birthday in hospital
  • 2013 - Passes away at home in Johannesburg at the age of 95

As Mandela’s life teaches us, only the concerted effort of all nations will empower humanity to respond to the enormous challenges that today threatens its very existence.”
